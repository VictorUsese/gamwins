The Different Types of Casino Bonuses Available 

Online casinos know that not every player is the same, therefore they devise different ways players can benefit from bonuses inside of their online casinos. Here’s a look at some of the different kinds of bonuses you might find inside your favorite online casinos.

Welcome Bonuses 

A Welcome Bonus is the first initial bonus offered to an online casino’s players when creating an account on their platform. These bonuses are typically the most lucrative to catch the eye of potential players and hook them into their online casino over their competitors.

These bonuses will often offer large deposit bonuses and even credit their players with Free Spins on top of matching their first deposit up to a certain amount. Here’s a look at what our top 5 picks offer their new players.

Deposit Bonuses

A deposit bonus can be triggered when making a new deposit into your online casino account. Keep an eye out for different promotions, as sometimes the banking method you choose can activate different types of bonuses. These bonuses typically come with a weekly limit on how many times you can claim.

Sticky Bonuses

A Sticky Bonus is often a bonus that can’t actually be removed from your account. Usually, the bonus funds awarded to your account come with strings attached to them, preventing you from withdrawing the amount unless you complete a certain requirement; such as a wagering requirement or rollover requirement. These bonuses often encourage players to wager the bonus funds up to a certain amount before it becomes withdrawable.

Free Spins

Free Spin bonuses are a great way for online casinos to highlight a newly released slot, or promote an existing slot on which they want to increase engagement. Free Spins will typically be granted in exchange for a qualifying deposit made into your account, and will then allow you to play a certain amount of spins on a selected video slot for free. Free Spins are often combined with other bonuses due to their popularity.

Reload Bonuses

A Reload Bonus works in a similar way to a Cashback bonus (see below), and will give you money back on deposits made in your online casino. Most Reload Bonuses are tied to a certain day of the week or period to encourage players to deposit at the start of the week and benefit from receiving a percentage of that deposit back as bonus funds.

Bonus Codes

Bonus Codes are often included in the terms of certain bonuses and promotions. When making a qualifying deposit on the Cashier page you must remember to use the Bonus Code when depositing to receive the Bonus. Online casinos will use Bonus Codes to differentiate their different bonuses and allow players to control which bonus they receive should there be multiple ongoing bonuses triggered by the same action, such as a deposit.

Cashback Bonuses

Cashback Bonuses are a way for the online casino to give money back to the player during their time playing in the casino. These bonuses are often linked to a player’s total net losses for a set period such as a week. The online casino will calculate how much you have lost in that period, and offer you a percentage of that amount back as bonus funds as a thank you for your loyalty.

Live Casino Bonuses

Live Casino Bonuses are pretty much exactly that. These bonuses will come in the same form as many of the other bonuses on the list but will be specific to the Live Casino section of the online casino. Best Bonus Guide: What You Should Check Before You Claim 

As mentioned above, every online casino comes with its own set of rules. To make sure you don’t get any unwanted surprises while participating in a promotion or claiming a bonus, make sure you check for these important bonus clauses.

Wagering Requirements

A wagering requirement is a stipulation attached to certain bonuses which makes the bonus funds inaccessible to withdraw until you have played those funds a certain amount of times. These requirements can range in value, and can often be snuck into bigger valued bonuses to reduce the chances of players actually getting their hands on those funds.

What you need to know about Wagering Requirements:

  • x1 is the lowest and usually for smaller bonuses for casual players
  • x25-x40 is around average for our top picks
  • Can go higher than x100 so make sure you check first

Let’s take a standard example:

40 x wagering requirement on a $100 Bonus would mean you need to play $4000 to meet the requirements.

Maximum Bet Using a Bonus

The maximum bet is the limit set by the casino on how much of the funds you can bet at one time. Usually, this is partnered with the wagering requirement to make sure you use the funds a certain way and across multiple different bets as opposed to all at once. Your winnings from bonuses can also be capped, as a casino doesn’t want you walking away with a million dollars from a free bonus, so make sure you read all the T&Cs.

What you need to know about Max. Bets:

  • The limit of the highest possible bet you can place with the bonus funds
  • Varies at each casino, so make sure you check each time
  • Ensures you spread your bets out a certain way and prevents you from placing one big bet

Bonus Validity

Bonus Validity is one of the most important clauses to look out for. After all, you need to know how long you have to use your bonus. The duration of every bonus is at the complete discretion of the online casino, so make sure you check if you have one hour to use your bonus, 2 weeks to use your bonus or longer. After this timeframe passes, any remaining bonus funds will be forfeited back to the online casino.

What you need to know about Bonus Validity:

  • The duration that the bonus is valid for
  • Once the time expires you will forfeit any remaining bonus funds
  • Bonus Validity can vary from bonus to bonus inside the same online casino

Game Bonus Contributions 

Playing on different parts of an online casino can contribute different percentages of your wager toward wagering requirements. For example, playing slots may count towards your wagering requirement 100%, meaning if you play $10 on slots then $10 is removed from your remaining wagering requirement. However, Blackjack may only count as 10%, meaning every $10 you play on Blackjack only counts as $1 off your remaining wagering requirement.

What you need to know about Game Contributions:

  • Always check the Game Bonus Contributions breakdown as it varies from bonus to bonus
  • Different types of casino games yield different contribution percentages

Withdrawal Processing Times

This is the amount of time expected to process your withdrawal request. This may also vary from bonus to bonus, so make sure you have a look at how long you should expect to wait before your withdrawal request will be processed and the funds can be transferred into your preferred banking method.

What you need to know about Withdrawal Processing Times:

  • The amount of time needed to process a withdrawal request
  • The length of time can vary for each bonus
