Classic Blackjack

With no fancy bows or ribbons, Classic Blackjack is for the purists and offers the game in its most common form. You’re dealt one hand, get as close to 21 without going bust, and beat the dealer.

Progressive or Jackpot Blackjack

The rules are very similar to Classic Blackjack, but the dealer is forced to hit on a score of 16 or lower and stand on 18 or above. These rules give an advantage to the players and make this variant popular with casuals.

European Blackjack

In this variant, the dealer will only deal himself his first card. The second card is only dealt to the dealer once the players have made their moves.

Switch Blackjack

In 2009, Blackjack Switch was created and introduced the rule of being dealt two cards instead of traditionally being dealt one. Players can also switch the top cards between their two hands during the initial deal.

Spanish Blackjack

Spanish Blackjack follows the same rules as Classic but it takes all the cards that score 10 points out of the deck making the scoring much lower and adding more importance on whether to stick or hit for another card.

How to Win at Blackjack on Mobile Apps

There are a few things you should always keep in mind when aiming to boost your bankroll playing Blackjack:

Have a Solid Strategy

Whilst Blackjack is a game of luck, it pays to understand the rules and develop a strategy to help you make clear decisions and not get carried away in the heat of the moment. First, let’s cover some of the main terminologies you may have heard in the movies.

Stand: Once you are happy with the cards you have you will tell the dealer you don’t want another card. This action is called sticking or standing.

Hit: Adding another card to your current hand is called a hit.

Bust: If your hand’s value exceeds 21 at any time during the round you will forfeit your hand as you have gone bust. Going bust causes you to lose your wager and lose the round.

Insurance: If a dealer’s face-up card is an Ace then you can place a side bet equal to half your original wager which is classed as insurance in case the dealer reveals a 21 score. Your insurance wager will pay out 2:1 if the dealer has 21.

Push: If both your hand and the dealer’s hand are tied then a push results, which returns your initial bet to you.

Surrender: Folding during a Blackjack game is uncommon, but certain variants will give you this option during the round to forfeit your hand. If the Dealer has an Ace at the time of your fold, you will get half your wager back.

As discussed above, there are different variations to the game Blackjack. Read the list carefully about the rule differences that each game variant has and decide which is the right one for you.
