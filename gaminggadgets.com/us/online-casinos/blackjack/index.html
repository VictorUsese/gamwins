Playing Blackjack: Basic Rules and Table Strategy

Playing blackjack is a game of strategy and skill. The game’s basic rules involve getting a hand total as close to 21 as possible without going over. Players are dealt two cards and can choose to “hit” and receive additional cards to improve their hand or “stand” and keep their current hand.

The ultimate goal is to beat the dealer’s hand without going over 21. The value of cards 2 through 10 corresponds to their face value, while aces can be worth 1 or 11, and face cards are worth 10. Therefore, it’s important to consider your table strategy while playing, such as when to hit, stand, split or double down, and know when the dealer is required to hit or stand.

Players should be familiar with the specific rules of the table they are playing at, such as whether the dealer hits on a soft 17 or not. Mastering basic rules and table strategy can increase your chances of winning and make the game more enjoyable.

Game Objective:

The objective of blackjack is to beat the dealer’s hand total without busting. If you exceed 21, you automatically lose. Many players believe that the goal is to form a two-card 21, a.k.a. blackjack or a multi-card 21 – but that’s not the case. Just beat the dealer, and you’re golden.

Extra Gameplay Options:

There are many different ‘actions’ available to blackjack players. These include the following: 

  • Hitting: A player chooses to take an additional card to improve their hand. The goal is simply to beat the dealer’s hand total without exceeding 21.
  • Standing: This is when a player decides to keep their current hand and not take any additional cards.
  • Doubling Down: This is when a player doubles their initial bet in exchange for one additional card. No further cards may be dealt.
  • Splitting: A player splits their hand into two separate hands, typically when they are dealt a pair or equal-value cards like 10-value cards.
  • Taking Insurance: This is when a player places an additional bet to protect themselves against the dealer getting a blackjack. When the dealer’s upcard is an Ace, players will have the option to take insurance.
  • Blackjack Side Bets: These are additional bets players can make on certain outcomes, such as getting a specific combination of cards. They are widely known as ‘sucker bets.’
  • Bet Behind: This feature allows players to place bets on other players’ hands.
  • Surrender: A player chooses to forfeit half of their bet and end their hand early, typically when the odds are not in their favor.

Be advised that not all blackjack variations will offer all of these betting options, and it’s essential to understand the rules of the table before making any bets. Players should also be aware that these additional bets may have a higher house edge, which means the chances of winning are lower.

Blackjack Terminology Explained

  • Hit: Take an additional card from the dealer to improve your hand.
  • Stand: Choosing not to take any more cards, keeping your current hand.
  • Split: When a player has two of the same cards or the same value cards, they can split them into two hands and play them individually.
  • Double Down: Doubling the original bet and taking only one more card.
  • Insurance: A side bet is offered when the dealer’s upcard is an Ace, which pays 2:1 if the dealer has a blackjack.
  • Surrender: Giving up a hand and forfeiting 50% of the original bet rather than playing the hand to completion.
  • Side Bets: Additional bets can be placed on certain outcomes, such as hitting a blackjack or various card combinations.
  • Push: A tie between the player and dealer, resulting in the player getting their bet back.
  • Bet: The amount of money placed on a hand or side bet before the start of a round.
  • Hole Card: The dealer’s face-down card is not shown to players until the end of a round.
